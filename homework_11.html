Brownian Motion (Wiener Process) — simulation via normal increments

We simulate a Brownian motion \(W_t\) on \([0,T]\) by partitioning time into \(n\) subintervals and summing independent Gaussian increments: \[ \Delta W_i \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\Delta t),\qquad \Delta t = \frac{T}{n}. \] Using the Box–Muller transform we convert uniform random numbers into standard normal samples, then scale them by \(\sqrt{\Delta t}\) to obtain the increments. Summing the increments gives a discrete path approximation of the continuous Wiener process.

1. Quick theory / reminders

  • \(W_0 = 0\).
  • Independent increments: \(W_{t+s}-W_t\) independent of past for \(s>0\).
  • Gaussian increments: \(W_{t+\Delta t}-W_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\Delta t)\).
  • Variance grows linearly: \(\mathrm{Var}(W_t)=t\) (for standard Brownian motion).

2. From Uniform to Normal — Box–Muller

Box–Muller: if \(U_1, U_2\sim\mathrm{Uniform}(0,1)\), then \[ Z_1 = \sqrt{-2\ln U_1}\cos(2\pi U_2),\qquad Z_2=\sqrt{-2\ln U_1}\sin(2\pi U_2) \] are independent \(\mathcal{N}(0,1)\) variables. Scale by \(\sqrt{\Delta t}\) to obtain \(\Delta W\).
