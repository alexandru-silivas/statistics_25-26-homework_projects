What is Statistics, and Why Can It Be Useful for Cybersecurity?

Course: Statistics — Sapienza University of Rome | Alexandru Silivas | 2025/2026

Statistics is the branch of mathematics concerned with collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data. It provides the tools necessary to understand variability in information, draw conclusions, and make informed decisions based on evidence rather than assumptions.

In the context of cybersecurity, statistics plays a vital role in detecting patterns, identifying anomalies, and modeling threats. By applying statistical techniques, analysts can measure the likelihood of cyberattacks, quantify risk, and recognize deviations in network activity that may indicate malicious behavior.
