Homework 7

Server updates as random walks and convergence to Binomial (n = 50)

Model: each week there are m independent attackers, each with breach probability p. A week is secure if no attacker succeeds (probability s=(1−p)m); otherwise it's breached. Assign +1 for a secure week, −1 for a breached week. Over n weeks the cumulative score S is the sum of those ±1 steps.

With K = number of secure weeks, K ~ Binomial(n, s) and final score S = 2K − n.

Panel A — Realistic: 1 attacker, p = 0.05 (80 sims)

Expected weekly secure probability s = (1 − p)m = 0.95. Final score should be high (positive drift).

Panel B — Balanced random walk (forced s = 0.5, 80 sims)

Here we force weekly survival probability to 0.5 (educational). This yields a symmetric random walk like the reference.

Panel C — Strong attacks: m = 50, p = 0.05 (400 sims)

Many attackers dramatically reduce s; the walk drifts downward. This is the realistic high-threat scenario.
