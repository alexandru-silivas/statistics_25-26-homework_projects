Assignment 1 — The Meaning of “Converge”

In probability theory, the phrase “the frequency converges to the probability” means that as we repeat an experiment many times, the observed frequency of an event approaches its theoretical probability. In simpler terms, the more data we gather, the closer our observations get to what probability theory predicts.

For example, if you toss a fair coin repeatedly, you might not get exactly 50% heads at first. But as the number of tosses grows into the hundreds or thousands, the proportion of heads will converge toward 0.5 — the true probability of getting heads.

Assignment 2 — The Law of Large Numbers

The Law of Large Numbers (LLN) is one of the foundational principles of probability and statistics. It states that as the number of independent experiments increases, the average of the observed results will get closer and closer to the expected (theoretical) value.

This means that randomness has less impact when data accumulates — the noise smooths out, and the true statistical pattern emerges. This principle is what allows us to make reliable predictions from data, power statistical inference, and ensure fair randomness in algorithms and cryptographic systems.
