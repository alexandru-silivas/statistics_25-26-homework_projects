Homework 8

Bernoulli processes vs Random walks — combinatorics and structure

This assignment explores the close relationship between a Bernoulli process and a random walk. A Bernoulli process is a sequence of independent trials with success probability p. If we map success → +1 and failure → −1, the cumulative sum is a random walk. The number of successes after n trials follows a Binomial(n, p) distribution.

What we will do

  1. Visually show convergence of empirical frequency to p (Law of Large Numbers — ties to HW4).
  2. Simulate random-walk trajectories and compare final-score histograms to the binomial PMF (ties to HW7).
  3. Display Pascal’s triangle (Tartaglia) and explain binomial coefficients, binomial expansion and brief links to the Fibonacci sequence.

1 — Bernoulli frequency convergence (interactive)

Run a sequence of Bernoulli trials and watch the running frequency of successes converge to p. This is the practical demonstration of the Law of Large Numbers (see Homework 4 for theory and experiments).

2 — Random walk trajectories & Binomial PMF

Choose n and p. We simulate multiple random-walk trajectories (success → +1, failure → −1) over n steps and show the histogram of final scores together with the theoretical binomial curve (converted to score-space).
